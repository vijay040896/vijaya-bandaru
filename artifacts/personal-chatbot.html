Meet Vijaya — A Personal Introduction Chatbot

Introduction

As part of the AI Lab (AIML-500) guided activity, I was tasked with building an AI chatbot or assistant using the Design Thinking framework — moving through Empathy, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test. I chose to build a personal-introduction chatbot: an AI assistant that lets visitors to my portfolio ask questions about me directly instead of scrolling through static text.

Description

The chatbot acts as a conversational version of my resume and bio. Visitors can ask it about my interests, my professional and academic background, my hobbies, and how to get in touch with me, and it responds conversationally, in real time, using information I provided about myself.

Work Sample — Representative Interaction:

Visitor: "What's your professional background?"

Assistant: "I'm an Appian Certified Senior Developer with 7+ years building low-code enterprise applications in regulated environments, and I'm currently studying AI and Data Analytics while exploring AI agent development, including a proof-of-concept code review pipeline using the Claude API."

Visitor: "How can I reach you?"

Assistant: "You can reach me at vijaybandaru040896@gmail.com or connect with me on LinkedIn — both are linked on the home page."

Objective

My goal was to give people visiting my portfolio a more interactive, engaging way to get to know me — instead of a static "About Me" page, they can ask exactly what they're curious about (my Appian background, my move into AI, how to reach me) and get a direct answer.

Process

I followed the Design Thinking cycle taught in the lab:

Empathy

I thought about who would actually visit my portfolio: recruiters, professors, and other professionals in the AI/data analytics space. I asked myself what they'd want to know quickly — my background, my current focus area, and how to contact me — and what would make that discovery feel less like reading a résumé and more like a conversation.

Define

I defined the problem as: static bios are easy to skim past. I wanted an assistant that could surface the right information depending on what the visitor actually cared about, rather than forcing everyone through the same fixed text.

Ideate

I brainstormed what the assistant should and shouldn't do: it should speak knowledgeably about my interests, experience, and hobbies, and clearly direct people to my contact information, but it shouldn't make up details about me or wander into unrelated topics.

Prototype

I built the assistant using Claude, giving it structured background information about myself (professional experience including my Appian work, my current academic focus in AI/data analytics, hobbies, and contact details) as instructions/context so it could answer naturally in conversation rather than reciting a script.

Test

I tested the assistant with the kinds of questions a visitor might realistically ask ("What's your background?", "What are you interested in?", "How can I reach you?") and refined the responses so they stayed accurate, on-topic, and in a natural conversational tone.

Tools and Technologies Used

Claude (Anthropic) — used to build and configure the conversational assistant with my personal and professional information as its knowledge base.

Value Proposition

Unique Value

This artifact demonstrates a skill directly tied to where I want to grow professionally — building AI agents and assistants — applied to something personal and low-stakes enough to experiment freely with design thinking before applying the same skills to more complex, professional use cases.

Relevance

Given my background in Appian and my goal of building a career in AI and AI agent development, this artifact is a tangible, interactive proof point: it shows I can take an idea from a defined user need through to a working conversational AI product, not just talk about AI conceptually.
